# WMT 2014

## What / Who

The **2014 Workshop on Statistical Machine Translation** benchmark — a standard dataset and evaluation for neural machine translation. Two tracks dominate:

- **WMT 2014 English-German (En-De)** — ~4.5M sentence pairs. `newstest2014` is the standard held-out test set; `newstest2013` is the dev set.
- **WMT 2014 English-French (En-Fr)** — ~36M sentence pairs. Much larger and considered easier in absolute BLEU terms.

## Significance

For roughly a decade after its release, WMT 2014 (especially En-De) was the dominant reference benchmark for neural MT research — an obligatory comparison table in nearly every new sequence-transduction architecture paper. It is the benchmark on which [[summary-attention-is-all-you-need|Attention Is All You Need]] reports its headline results, and the one that certified the [[transformer]] as a credible new architecture:

- Transformer (big): **28.4 BLEU** on En-De — new SOTA, +2.0 BLEU over the previous best (which was itself an ensemble).
- Transformer (big): **41.8 BLEU** on En-Fr — new single-model SOTA.
- Training cost: 3.5 days on 8× P100 GPUs — a small fraction (roughly 1/50×) of the compute used by prior SOTA ensembles.

The efficiency story on WMT'14 was arguably as influential as the BLEU story: a small team could retrain and iterate on the Transformer in days, not weeks. That changed the practical rhythm of MT research.

## Timeline / Notable Work

- **2014** — WMT workshop held, datasets released.
- **2017** — Transformer (Vaswani et al.) sets SOTA on both En-De and En-Fr tracks.
- **2017–2023** — Standard benchmark in nearly every published MT architecture paper. Gradually superseded by newer benchmarks (FLORES, WMT 2020+, multi-lingual evaluations) as the field moved to larger, more multilingual settings.
