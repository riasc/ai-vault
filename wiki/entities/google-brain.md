# Google Brain

## What / Who

Google's flagship deep-learning research group, founded in 2011 as a Google X project under Jeff Dean, Greg Corrado, and Andrew Ng. Merged with DeepMind in April 2023 to form **Google DeepMind**, Google's unified AI research organization.

## Significance

Google Brain is the institutional home of most of the authors of [[summary-attention-is-all-you-need|Attention Is All You Need]] (2017), where the original [[transformer]] was designed, implemented, and trained. Several authors are listed as "Google Research" rather than "Google Brain" specifically — reflecting the overlapping organizational structure of Google's AI research at the time — but the work was centered at Brain.

Beyond the Transformer, Google Brain is responsible for or heavily involved in many landmark deep-learning contributions: TensorFlow, the tensor2tensor framework (the codebase used to train the Transformer), sequence-to-sequence learning (Sutskever 2014), word2vec (Mikolov 2013), mixture-of-experts layers, and Google Translate's neural machine translation stack.

## Timeline / Notable Work

- **2011** — Founded as a Google X project.
- **2014** — Sequence-to-sequence learning (Sutskever, Vinyals, Le).
- **2015** — TensorFlow released as open source.
- **2017** — Attention Is All You Need; the Transformer.
- **2023** — Merged with DeepMind to form Google DeepMind.
