# Arc Institute

## What / Who

**Arc Institute** is an independent biomedical research institute based in Palo Alto, California, founded in 2021. It operates outside the traditional university and pharmaceutical-industry models, with internal funding (notably backed by Patrick Collison, Vitalik Buterin, and others) and a focus on long-horizon biomedical research.

## Significance

Arc Institute is the lead institutional home of the [[evo-2]] project and the broader Evo series of biological foundation models. Both [[brian-hie|Brian Hie]] and [[patrick-hsu|Patrick Hsu]] — co-senior authors of [[summary-evo-2]] — are core investigators at Arc.

Arc represents a deliberate experiment in research institute design: combining the autonomy of a private lab with the open-publication norms of academic biology. The Evo series is one of its highest-profile outputs and demonstrates the model's appeal — large-scale ML-for-biology work that's hard to do inside either a university (compute and engineering constraints) or a pharma company (open-release norms don't match commercial incentives).

## Timeline / Notable Work

- **2021** — Founded.
- **2024** — Evo 1 published in *Science* (Nguyen et al.). First large prokaryotic DNA foundation model.
- **2026** — Evo 2 published in *Nature* (Brixi et al.) — see [[summary-evo-2]]. 40B-parameter cross-domain DNA foundation model.
- Maintains open tools at `arcinstitute.org/tools/evo/` including `evo-designer` (sequence generation/scoring web interface) and `evo-mech-interp` (SAE feature exploration).
